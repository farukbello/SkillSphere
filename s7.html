Carpentry Training Program Summary

Goal: To equip carpenters with both theoretical knowledge and practical skills, from basic tool use to advanced furniture-making techniques, ensuring they are capable of creating quality wooden products and maintaining their tools safely.

Module 1: Introduction to Carpentry Tools & Materials

Tools:

  • Hand tools: Hammer, saw, chisel, measuring tape, square.
  • Power tools: Circular saw, jigsaw, drill.
  • Safety Equipment: Gloves, goggles, ear protection.

Materials:

  • Softwood (Pine, Cedar): Easy to cut, commonly used for furniture.
  • Hardwood (Oak, Mahogany): Stronger, used for more durable pieces.
  • Manufactured materials: Plywood and MDF for economical furniture.

Practical:

Trainees practice using basic tools, cutting wood, and familiarizing themselves with different materials.

Module 2: Basic Carpentry Techniques

Joinery:

  • Butt Joint: Simple connection of two wood pieces.
  • Dovetail & Mortise and Tenon Joints: Stronger joints for high-quality furniture.

Measuring and Marking:

Correct measurement and accurate marking are key to precise cuts.

Practical:

Learners practice cutting wood, creating basic joints (butt joints), and learning the importance of precise measuring and marking.

Module 3: Furniture Making Techniques

Furniture Design:

Consider functionality (e.g., does it work for its purpose?), size/shape, and aesthetics (how should it look?).

Building a Simple Table or Chair:

Learn how to build basic furniture (table, chair) by cutting, joining, and finishing the pieces. Understand the process from start to finish, including measurements, cuts, and assembly.

Practical:

Hands-on construction of simple furniture like a table or chair, from cutting wood to assembly and finishing.

Module 4: Advanced Carpentry Skills

Custom Furniture Making:

Designing and building unique pieces like bookshelves or custom cabinets.

Advanced Joints:

Learning complex joints like dovetail or box joints for higher-quality and more intricate furniture.

Practical:

Build custom projects such as bookshelves or cabinets, focusing on creating complex joints and applying professional techniques.
